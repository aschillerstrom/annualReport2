Bringing the Court to the Community

- Provided mobile court opportunities at the Willie Mae Kirk, Old Quarry, Cepeda Branch, and Pleasant Hill Branch libraries enabling citizens to meet with clerks and judges to resolve their cases.

- Conducted quarterly Driver's License Clinics in conjunction with Texas Fair Defense and The University of Texas School of Law providing relief to citizens whose driver license suspensions cause them to face possible incarceration and employment barriers.

- Organized assessments and taught classes for Youth Services at Little Walnut Creek, Ruiz Branch, Southeast Branch, University Hills Branch, Cepeda Branch, Terrazas Branch, Pleasant Hill Branch, Twin Oaks Branch libraries and the Gus Garcia Recreation Center alleviating mobility concerns of families in need of services.

- Implemented a text messaging service reminding citizens of their upcoming court dates.

Providing Economic Opportunity and Removing Barriers to Employment via Judicial Order

- Approved 15,834 cases for deferred disposition allowing those charged with criminal violations to pay a fine and complete a term of up to 6 months without further violations to have their case dismissed without a convicion appearing on their record.

- Granted 28,566 extensions to pay enabling citizens additional time to pay their fines and fees and to avoid a violation of the terms of their cases and a potential warrant.

- Permitted 29,697 extensions to comply or show proof giving citizens the opportunity to comply with the terms of their cases to have the cases dismissed.

- Waived Department of Public Safety surcharges for 934 citizens, allowing them to receive a driver's license so they can resume work, education and other activities without fear of violating the law by driving without a license.

Providing a Government that Works for All by Understanding and Overcoming Financial Barriers

- Provided free Alive@25 and Basic Traffic classes to over 200 youth

- Placed 13,808 cases on payment plans giving citizens a customized and affordable path to compliance.

- Accepted $511,908 in Community Service in lieu of monetary payment relieving the financial burden caused by a citation.

- Authorized $4,698,104 in Jail Credit in lieu of monetary payment allowing citizens a clean slate to restart their lives.

- Waived $902,365 of fines and fees via judicial order ensuring fairness and equity to Austin's vulnerable citizens.

- Forgave $240,303 in warrant fees via judicial ordered during Warrant Amnesty event encouraging citizens to come back into compliance with their cases.

Encouraging Responsibility to Make a Safer Community

- Dismissed 5,440 cases when citizens came into compliance by registering their car, providing proof of insurance, renewing, updating, or receiving their driver's license, purchasing a child seat, or repairing equipment violations.

- Educated 12,651 citizens on traffic safety via the Driver Safety Course deferred disposition program ensuring a knowledge of traffic laws and safe driving practices.

- Brought 245 citizens and businesses into compliance with the Code and Health Department's ordinances via deferred disposition by requiring legal conformance as a term of dismissal.

- Dismissed an additional 113 Code and Health Department violation cases due to citizens and business complying with ordinances prior to court date.

Close

Building Trust Through Community Involvement

- Partnered with Austin Police Department's Police Activity League and the Texas Municipal Court Education Center to create driving course using the STAR Cart and Impaired Driving goggles to provide realistic impaired driving simulation.

- Collaborated with SafeKids to demonstrate child safety seat protocol and provided appointments for hands-on car seat training.

- Taught importance of traffic safety at city-wide summer camps

- Partnered with AustinCorps to provide an overview of court operations and the importance of traffic safety laws to high school students

- Distributed traffic safety materials to over 300 citizens during National Night Out events

- Engaged with Texas A & M Agri-life Extension Program to provide distracted driving simulation

- Hosted Gina Newton from Andrew's Choice who spoke with teen drivers about the loss of her son due to an accident caused by speeding.

- Participated in Coats for Kids Community Fair and the Build-a-Backpack campaign providing much needed clothing and supplies to the community.

- Participated in the City of Austin's Safety Conference providing driving safety materials to City employees as well as providing an impaired driving simulation.

- Surveyed citizens at the Court to receive feedback on a myriad of topics ranging from their perception of receiving fair and impartial justice to modes of transportation utilized to their overall customer experience.
